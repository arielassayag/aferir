# ABSTRACT

Constitutional Amendment 132/2023 is replacing five Brazilian taxes with a
dual
VAT governed by three reference rates, one federal (CBS), one state and one
municipal (IBS), to be set by the Senate upon TCU calculations that rely on
tax-secrecy-protected data. Using exclusively open data, this paper
anticipates the three reference rates through a deterministic public routine
that mirrors the legal architecture of the official calculation (LC
214/2025, arts. 349-369), and estimates the state and municipal split
of the IBS under the enacted legislation. Unconstrained, the estimated sum
exceeds the 26.5% statutory review trigger (art. 475, §11), as official
estimates also do; applying the adjustment the law itself mandates, the
compatible rates are

{{csv:trava_conforme.csv:cenario_gamma=central:tau_M_pp:.1f}} percentage
points, at the cost of cutting
{{csv:trava_conforme.csv:cenario_gamma=central:lambda:.0%}} of preferential
regimes. Under the 2033 legal distribution, median entity-level sufficiency
is {{csv:metricas_dist.csv:chave=suf_mediana:valor:.1f}}%, and the boundary
between open and administrative data is mapped sphere by sphere.
